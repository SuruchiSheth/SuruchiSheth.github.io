CMU Raasta

Raas All-Stars VIII: The National Championship

1st place performance by CMU Raasta

Captain 2015-2016

In my final year as a dancer on CMU Raasta, I stepped up as one of the co-captains for the team. My co-captain and I were the primary leads for all of the choreography, formations, logistics, finances, costumes, theme prep, and mix for the new season. This was the first year the the team transitioned into a new set of captains. Because of our success in the 2014-2015 year, we had gained much recognition for the Carnegie Mellon community and were able to perform and several on-campus events. We successfully brought on 10 new members and competed at three bid competitions. Once again, we ranked third in the national and were invited to attend Raas All-Stars: The National Championship. The competition was held on April 23, 2016, and CMU Raasta was proud to bring home the gold, as the 2015-2016 National Garba-Raas Champions.

Vice Captain 2014-2015

The 2014-2015 season was a game-changer for CMU Raasta. Three years of hard work finally helped us accomplish our dreams - attending Raas All-Stars: The National Championship. We started choreography in the summer of 2014 and continued our efforts through the spring. We attended three competitions, and because of our success, were ranked third in the nation, qualifying for the national championship in Houston, TX. This has been a personal dream of mine since I attended my first Raas competition as an audience member in 8th grade. Despite overcoming several obstacles, my team proved to me that anything is possible, as long as you work hard, work passionately, and work together as a family. It was the first set of placings ever in the history of this team, since its establishment in 2012. Because of our success, we were also able to gain tremendous financial support from the Carnegie Mellon community to help us attend the nationals. We also blew the Garba-Raas community away, as an underdog team that they did not expect to be successful.

Bhangra in the Burgh

Bhangra in the Burgh (BIB) is a non-profit entirely student-run bhangra competition affiliated with Carnegie Mellon University. A platform for top Bhangra teams from across the nation to showcase their talent in a colorful exhibition of the energy and excitement, BIB has grown exponentially and currently stands as the largest student-run event in Pittsburgh. Over the past nine years, Bhangra in the Burgh has successfully attracted a sold-out crowd of 2,300 each year and raised a total of over $70,000 for charity. See: http://bhangraintheburgh.org

CO-DIRECTOR 2015-2016

The co-chair position is the highest on the executive board, directly handling all finances and official contact between Carnegie Mellon Student Activities, teams, the e-board, and the student staff. As a member of the executive board for both BIB6 and BIB7, this was the final step for me in my involvement with the organization.

As one of three co-chairs/co-directors of Bhangra in the Burgh 8, it was my job to oversee the Public Relations, Pittsburgh Liaison, Webmaster, and Design Chairs. Preparation began in March as we interviewed candidates for various executive board positions. Throughout the summer, we worked to get a website up and application packets out to teams. We also invited a Chai Town A capella from the University of Illinois at Urbana-Champaign, the first famous group to perform at BIB as an exhibition act in history. The next several months involved logistics and management for team hospitality, emcee selection, show operations, as well as several contracts and planning for the venue Soldiers and Sailors Memorial Hall. The 3-hour show was held on November 1, 2015, with a crowd of about 2,500 families and students from the Pittsburgh community. With a $25,000 budget for the show, we were  able to donate over $7,000 of proceeds to the Homeless Childrens Education fund.

 

 

 

 

PR CHAIR 2014-2015

During my sophomore year at CMU, I was selected to be the Public Relations chair for Bhangra in the Burgh 7. The public relations campaign for Bhangra in the Burgh is very thorough, as there is much pressure to sell out the show and donate as much as possible to the local charity.

My co-chair and I were in charge of social media PR, active on-campus and off-campus PR, and developing new, creative PR campaigns. We took the campaign in a different direction this year to raise more awareness. We developed spirit competitions in order to get the Carnegie Mellon crowd more excited for the show, and also have more attendees outside of Pittsburgh. This was also the first year BIB used Instagram and utilized a #burgh7 campaign. I also took it upon myself to make a teaser video for BIB7 and the staff video that was shown at the end of the show. After the show, we received feedback from many alumni, saying that the PR campaign this year was better than ever before. The tactics we developed were used for BIB8, BIB9, and will be used for the upcoming BIBX.
